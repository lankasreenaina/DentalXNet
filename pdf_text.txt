DentalXNet: Automated Detection of Dental Treatments in OPG Radiographs using Hybrid CNN –Transformer 
Architecture 
 
Abstract 
Panoramic dental X-rays, also called Orthopantomograms (OPG) are commonly used in dentistry to diagnose and assess 
dental treatments like crowns, implants, bridges, fillings and root canal procedures.Manual analysis of these X -rays 
requires professionals and can be time-consuming especially in busy clinics. Also having treatments in one image and 
differences in body structures make manual analysis difficult. This study suggests DentalXNet an automated system that 
uses intelligence to detect multiple dental treatments in panoramic X-rays. It uses a combination of technologies to 
extract features focus on details and understand the context. We. Tested DentalXNet using a dataset of 2235 annotated 
OPG X-rays with six treatment categories. Our baseline model that uses YOLOv8 is able to get it about 47 percent of the 
time. We are using YOLOv8 for our baseline model. The accuracy is around 0.47 when we use YOLOv8  . Adding an 
attention module improved the performance to about 0.62. The final model, RT -DETR achieved a precision of 0.853, 
recall of 0.638 and accuracy of 0.713. The results show that using transformer -based detection significantly improves 
accuracy by considering the X-ray image. DentalXNet has potential as a tool to help doctors analyze dental X -rays 
automatically. The proposed system can help dentists diagnose and evaluate treatments more efficiently. Panoramic 
dental radiographs are widely used in dentistry. Dentalxnet can assist in their analysis. Dental treatments such as crowns, 
implants and bridges can be detected using DentalXNet. The accuracy of DentalXNet makes it a valuable tool, for 
diagnosis. This work demonstrates a scalable and interpretable AI-based solution for automated dental radiograph 
analysis with potential real-world clinical applicability. 
 
1. Introduction 
Dental panoramic radiographs are really important for dentists to check health and figure out what treatments are needed 
like crowns, implants, bridges, fillings and root canals. These radiographs show the jaw so dentists can look at lots of 
teeth at the same time. But it can be tough to interpret these radiographs by hand because the images are big there are lots 
of overlapping structures and peoples anatomy is all different. Dental clinics that see a lot of patients need to look at 
radiographs which makes manual analysis take too long and prone to mistakes. There have been some improvements in 
using Artificial Intelligence and deep learning to analyze medical images. Convolutional Neural Networks have been 
really good at finding patterns in images. Recently transformer-based architectures have gotten a lot better at finding 
relationships between different parts of images. Some frameworks, like YOLO, Faster R -CNN and Mask R-CNN have 
been used a lot in imaging to find structures and abnormalities. These detec tors mostly focus on small parts of the image 
and might have trouble understanding how the whole jaw works in panoramic radiographs.  
So this study is proposing a way of doing things a hybrid framework that uses Convolutional Neural Networks and 
transformer-based modeling to look at dental panoramic radiographs. This framework combines the things about 
convolutional feature extraction and transformer-based contextual modeling to understand dental panoramic radiographs 
better. 
Contributions of the Study 
The main contributions of this project include: 
1. It makes a system that can automatically find different dental treatments in panoramic radiographs.  
2.  It uses something called Squeeze-and-Excitation attention to make the features that the computer looks at 
better. 
3. It uses a kind of detector called RT-DETR transformer to find objects in pictures and understand the context.  
4.  It checks how well the model works by looking at things, like precision and recall and mAP.  
5. It shows what the model thinks and where it is looking by making pictures of the predictions and attention heat 
maps so we can understand how it works 
Novelty of the Proposed Work 
The novelty of this work lies in the integration of convolutional neural networks, attention mechanisms, and transformer -
based detection into a unified framework for panoramic dental image analysis. Unlike traditional approaches that rely 
solely on CNN architectures, the proposed DentalXNet leverages global contextual understanding through RT -DETR 
while enhancing feature representation using Squeeze-and-Excitation attention. Additionally, the inclusion of 
explainability through attention visualization and Grad-CAM further distinguishes this work by improving 
interpretability in medical imaging applications. 
2. Literature Review 
Recent research has looked into using learning for analyzing dental X-rays. Several studies have focused on finding teeth 
identifying work and detecting problems using a type of artificial intelligence called convolutional neural networks.  
• Early work by Tuzoff et al. (2019) used a type of AI called CNN to detect and number teeth in X-ray images. 
Their approach showed that automated dental analysis using learning is possible. Yüksel et al. In 2021 created a 
deep learning system that can detect dental treatments in X-ray images. The results were mainly relied on CNN 
architectures. 
• Similarly Çelik and Çelik (2022) proposed a learning approach for detecting dental restorations in X-ray images. 
The model had accuracy but struggled with detecting small dental structures. Choi et al. In 2022 looked into 
detecting dental treatment patterns using convolutional neural networks. Their model successfully identified 
dental procedures but had trouble capturing the bigger picture. 
• In another study Altan et al. In 2022 applied intelligence for detecting prosthetic dental restorations. While their 
model performed well it required a lot of preprocessing and large datasets. Recently transformer -based models 
have gained attention in medical imaging tasks. Dosovitskiy et al. In 2020 introduced Vision Transformers, 
which can capture long-range dependencies in images. 
• Carion et al. In 2020 proposed the Detection Transformer (DETR) which replaced detection pipelines with 
transformer architectures. DETR showed performance in object detection tasks. More recent studies have 
explored architectures combining CNNs with transformers. Alsakar et al. In 2024 proposed a -label dental 
disorder classification system using MobileNetV2 and Swin Transformer.  
• Similarly Li and Zhang in 2024 introduced a transformer-based -label classification model for dental panoramic 
images. Raeisi and Rokhva in 2024 explored attention mechanisms for -label dental condition detection. Other 
studies have investigated object detection frameworks such as YOLO in healthcare imaging (Palaniappan et al. 
2025). Several works have also applied YOLO-based detection for anomaly detection (Şevik & Mutlu 2025). A 
systematic review by Cai et al. In 2025 summarized the applications of YOLO a rchitectures in imaging. 
Additionally, Singh et al. In 2025 introduced a transformer-based YOLO architecture, for anomaly detection. 
 
Research Gap 
There are still some problems with the research that has been done far. 
1. A lot of studies only use models that are based on CNN, which might not be good enough to understand the big 
picture in panoramic images. 
2. It is still hard to find things like endodontic posts and root canal fillings in dental images.  
3. Not many people have looked into using both CNN and Transformer models to detect the different kinds of 
dental treatments. 
4. Many models do not have a way to explain how they are making their decisions, such, as showing what they are 
paying attention to. 
This study is trying to fix these problems by using attention mechanisms and transformer -based detection models to 
make dental treatment detection better. 
 
3. Proposed Methodology 
System Architecture 
The proposed DentalXNet framework follows a hybrid deep learning pipeline consisting of three main stages: 
1. Feature Extraction using CNN backbone 
2. Attention-based feature enhancement 
3. Transformer-based object detection 
Workflow of the Proposed System 
1. Input panoramic radiograph 
2. Image preprocessing 
3. Data augmentation 
4. CNN feature extraction 
5. Attention-based feature refinement 
6. Transformer detection module 
7. Bounding box prediction and classification 
 

Training Configuration 
The models were trained using a pre-trained backbone with transfer learning to improve convergence. Training was 
performed using the Adam optimizer with appropriate learning rate scheduling. The input image resolution was fixed at 
640×640 pixels, and training was conducted for multiple epochs to ensure stable learning. Batch processing and GPU 
acceleration (Tesla T4) were utilized to optimize computational efficiency.  
Model Components 
CNN Backbone 
The CNN backbone looks at the picture. Finds things like edges and shapes and more complicated things like teeth and 
dental implants. It does this by using convolution layers. The CNN backbone is very important because it helps the 
model understand what is in the picture. 
Attention Mechanism (SE Block) 
The Squeeze-and-Excitation module is like a helper that makes the model pay attention to the things that're important. It 
does this by giving weight to the things that the model should focus on like metallic implants and bridges. This helps the 
model find the things that it needs to find. 
Transformer Detection Model 
The last part of the model uses something called RT-DETR. This is a kind of model that uses attention to understand how 
things are related to each other in the picture. The Transformer Detection Model looks at the picture and finds the 
relationships between teeth and the things around them. The model can understand things like how teeth're connected to 
each other and to other things in the picture. The Transformer Detection Model is very good, at finding things in the 
picture because it uses the CNN backbone and the Attention Mechanism to help it.

Discussion on Clinical Relevance 
The improved precision achieved by the RT-DETR model is particularly significant in clinical scenarios where false 
positives can lead to incorrect diagnosis or unnecessary treatment planning. The model demonstrates strong performance 
in detecting high-density structures such as implants and bridges, which are critical indicators in dental assessments. 
Although recall is slightly lower compared to CNN-based models, the trade-off favors reliability over over-detection, 
making the system more suitable for real-world deployment in dental diagnostics. 
 
Final Benchmark Conclusion 
The proposed DentalXNet significantly outperforms traditional CNN-based detection models by integrating attention 
mechanisms and transformer-based global context modeling. While introducing moderate computational overhead, the 
model achieves superior precision and detection reliability, making it well-suited for real-world clinical applications. 
Compared to existing literature based on the same dataset, this work demonstrates clear improvements in detection 
accuracy, architectural design, and interpretability, establishing it as a strong candidate for conference -level research.

This study is about something called DentalXNet which's a new way to use computers to automatically find dental work 
in big pictures of teeth. We found out that using attention mechanisms and transformer architectures makes the detector 
work a lot better than older ways of doing it. The final model we made called RT-DETR was really good, at finding lots 
of kinds of dental work and it got a score of 0.713, which is very good. This system could really help dentists by looking 
at pictures of teeth and finding problems automatically which would save time and make diagnoses more accurate.  
In the future we might try to add pictures to our collection use 3D pictures and make a system that can help dentists make 
decisions in real time using DentalXNet and the RT-DETR model to find dental treatments. 
This research presented DentalXNet, a hybrid CNN–Transformer framework for automated detection of dental 
treatments in panoramic dental radiographs. 
The study demonstrated that integrating attention mechanisms and transformer architectures significantly improves 
detection performance compared to traditional CNN-based detectors. 
The final RT-DETR model achieved an mAP50 of 0.713, demonstrating strong performance in detecting multiple dental 
treatments. 
The proposed system has the potential to support dental professionals by providing automated analysis of panoramic 
radiographs, reducing diagnostic time and improving accuracy. 
Future work may focus on expanding the dataset, incorporating 3D imaging modalities such as CBCT, and developing 
real-time clinical decision support systems. 
7. Future Scope 
Future work can focus on enhancing the detection of low-contrast and small dental structures such as endodontic posts 
through specialized feature enhancement techniques. The integration of tooth -level localization and segmentation can 
further improve clinical usability. Additionally, optimizing the model for lightweight deployment can enable real -time 
applications in clinical environments. Expanding the dataset with diverse and multi -center radiographs, along with 
incorporating 3D imaging modalities such as CBCT, can further strengthen the robustness and generalization capability 
of the proposed system.
